Logistic (Sigmoid) function/ regression vectorized implementation of theta (parameters) update:
theta := theta - (alpha/m)*X'*(g(X*theta) - y),
where theta is the updated parameters vector (to the left of the equals sign),
theta is the current parameters vector (to the right of the equals sign),
alpha is the learning rate,
m is the count of the training sample,
X' - the transpose of the input data (transposed matrix of independent variables),
g(X*theta) - the predicted value of y given the input data X and the current parameters (theta vector) of our hypothesis function,
y - the real classification (1 or 0)/ dependent variable;

Cost function of Logistic regression:
J(theta) = - (1/m)*Riemann sum(y*log(h(x)) + (1-y)*log(1 - h(x)))
Cost function (vectorized implementation):
h = g(X*theta);
J(theta) = (1/m)*(-y'*log(h)-(1-y)'*log(1-h)),
where J - cost function,
theta - parameters vector,
m - number of observations in the training sample,
y - the vector of dependent variable's observations,
h - our hypothesis given input data (X) and parameters (theta),
